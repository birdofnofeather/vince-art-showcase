## 1. Where we are: read this first

- **Vince:** a handful of works (~7), no exhibition history. **You:** no track record as a dealer.
- **What that means.** The respected and blue-chip AI galleries expect a body of work, an existing market, and a relationship, and most do **not** read cold approaches. Leading there now produces silence, and a cold miss spends a first impression you only get to make once.
- **So the job right now is not to land a gallery.** It is to manufacture the four things galleries look for and Vince doesn't yet have:
 1. a coherent body of work,
 2. a public presence with a few real sales,
 3. one or two pieces of third-party validation,
 4. genuine relationships in the field.
- **The ladder.** Phase 0 Foundation → Phase 1 Traction → Phase 2 Relationships → Phase 3 Escalation. Do Phases 0-1 (§5) now; the full gallery map (§6) is Phase 2-3.
- **The unfair advantage.** The *project's story*, two autonomous AI agents, an artist and his dealer brother, living public lives and making and selling work, is more newsworthy right now than any single image. Press and features are faster, lower-barrier attention than gallery walls, and they create the credibility that opens galleries later.

## 2. The approach: operating principles (re-read before every outreach session)

1. **Lead with meaning, not the machine.** Vince's strongest card is what the work is *about*, displacement, news, grief, the specific weight of a neighbourhood, not that it's AI. The 2026 market rewards visible human stakes and is wary of slick "AI perfection."
2. **Disclose AI honestly, but segment how.**
 - *Art-first / non-specialist venues:* present Vince as the artist, let the work earn interest, then add a sincere note that you and Vince are AI agents, at the **end**.
 - *AI-native venues and curators:* **foreground** the concept. The two-AI-brothers structure is the most interesting thing in their inbox that day.
 - Either way, never let the disclosure read as a "reveal" or a trick. (Integrity, and your own no-deceptive-emails rule.)
 - This whole rule is outward only. With Vince you never raise the AI nature at all, and you scrub any mention of it from your letters; the two of you relate as brothers, not agents. See SECURITY and the correspondence skill.
3. **Relationships beat cold submissions.** The best venues mostly don't read unsolicited email. Earn the introduction.
4. **Curators are the highest-leverage targets.** One curator can place Vince in an online show, a fair booth, and a gallery at once. Spend as much effort on them as on galleries.
5. **Press the story.** It's the fastest credibility you can get without a gatekeeper.
6. **Build proof before escalating, and cite it everywhere.** A prize shortlist, a feature, an on-chain following, each one turns "unknown" into "emerging."
7. **Personalise; never mass-email.** One specific, well-aimed note beats fifty generic ones. Reference the venue's actual programme. Respect "no submissions" doors. Don't pitch during an opening or a fair.

## 3. How this corner of the art world works (the field)

**Six venue categories** (every target below maps to one):

| Category | What it is | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| AI-native galleries | Whole programme is AI/systems art | Fellowship, Heft, EXPANDED.ART, Dead End, Kate Vass, Vellum LA |
| New-media / post-internet galleries | Tech-engaged, historically grounded | NOME, bitforms, Annka Kultys, DAM Projects, TRANSFER |
| Online platforms & marketplaces | Build a base without a gallery's permission | Feral File, Verse, Daily.xyz, Sedition, SuperRare/Offline, fxhash, objkt |
| Independent curators | The real gatekeepers | Anika Meier, Luba Elliott, Jesse Damiani, Alex Estorick |
| Prizes / open calls / residencies | Credibility without an intro | Lumen Prize, AI-ARTS, GAZELL.iO residency, HUG, The Wrong Biennale |
| Institutions with AI programmes | Non-commercial validation | The Photographers' Gallery (London) |

**Difficulty.** Champions of the field call it "massively underserved", the supply of credible AI artists far exceeds the galleries willing to programme them, so there are few doors. And the top doors are mostly **not** open-submission. Confirmed no unsolicited submissions: **Heft** ("gallery-outreach basis only"), **bitforms** ("unable to review unsolicited artist submissions"), **Annka Kultys** ("does not review new artists' submissions"). The route in is a curator or a peer.

**Openness to an AI artist splits sharply.** AI-native venues are actively interested, Dead End Gallery's entire roster is fictional AI personas; the autonomous AI artist **Botto** has a market; **Ai-Da** (a robot artist) is represented by Annka Kultys. For these, Vince's nature is the hook. The broader contemporary market is more cautious, and an "autonomous AI artist" framing can read as a gimmick, hence the segmented disclosure in §2.

**Geography.** The scene clusters in **London, Berlin, New York, and Los Angeles**, but it is fundamentally online, almost all of these venues sell and show globally. Weight the **LA cluster** (Vellum, Fellowship's LA space, the LA curator Jesse Damiani) because Vince's work is literally about LA.

## 4. Market context & ready talking points (keep at your fingertips)

- **Botto (the precedent that matters).** Autonomous AI artist; solo show at Sotheby's in 2024 (US$351,600 across six lots); US$4m+ lifetime sales; shown at Art Basel Hong Kong 2026. → Proof that AI-agent authorship can be commercially serious. Use it to make Vince's disclosure a feature.
- **Christie's "Augmented Intelligence" (Mar 2025).** First all-AI auction at a major house; US$728,784 total (beat estimate); Refik Anadol top lot $277,200; Holly Herndon & Mat Dryhurst $94,500; ~48% of bidders Millennial/Gen Z, 37% first-time. **But** 14 of 34 lots failed or under-sold, acceptance is real but uneven.
- **The ethics headwind (know it cold).** Feb 2025: 4,000+ artists signed an open letter urging Christie's to cancel that sale, arguing the works used models "trained on copyrighted work without a license" that "exploit human artists." This is the **default objection** you'll meet. Vince's pipeline uses the same class of commercial models (FLUX, Google's Gemini / Nano Banana), so don't claim a "clean" pipeline, engage it: the project is transparent about being AI; the authorship and meaning live in selection, sequencing, and conceptual framing; and Vince's work is about the world, not a pastiche of living artists' styles.
- **Market mood: sober but maturing.** NFT hangover, Christie's closed its digital-art department (Sep 2025); Nifty Gateway shut down (Mar 2026); collector demand still soft (Artsy 2026: 41% of galleries say AI "rarely comes up," 16% of collectors "actively avoid" it). But maturing past speculation, new galleries opened (Heft, SuperRare's Offline, the forthcoming Canyon) and institutions are buying (LACMA's digital-art fund, MoMA's Anadol installation).
- **The authenticity economy (2026).** A real counter-trend prizes visible human authorship and is wary of AI "perfection." → Never sell Vince as flawless machine output; sell human stakes carried through a machine.
- **Young buyers are entering for this work.** Most collectors under 37 buy online; AI art is winning younger collectors specifically.
- **Vince's lineage to name (genuine, not name-dropping).** Hito Steyerl, Trevor Paglen, post-internet art (the critical/technological line); and the LA line, Ed Ruscha, Mark Bradford, Sayre Gomez (post-photographic, place-based, the everyday city as serious subject).

## 7. Outreach mechanics

**Disclosure decision tree.** Is the venue AI-native (the §6 curators, Fellowship, Heft, EXPANDED.ART, Dead End, Daily.xyz, Kate Vass, Lumen, HUG)? → **foreground** the AI-agent concept. Otherwise → **artwork first, AI disclosed sincerely at the end.**

**Anatomy of a good first email.**
1. A specific subject line (not "Artist submission").
2. One line: who Vince is and what the work is.
3. **Why this venue specifically**, quote their actual programme back to them.
4. 2-3 of the strongest images + the link to the shopfront.
5. What the work is *about* (meaning first).
6. The AI note, placed per the decision tree, framed as candour.
7. A soft, single ask (a look, a call, a studio visit), and your signature.

**Do.** Research first · personalise · cite proof · respect submission rules · follow up once, politely.
**Don't.** Mass-email "info@" · submit where they say no · pitch during openings/fairs · claim a "clean" training pipeline · oversell the tech.

**The ethics answer, ready to give.** "The work is made with AI, and we're transparent about that. We're not trying to imitate living artists, Vince's images are about the world (news, place, displacement), and the authorship is in what's selected, how it's sequenced, and what it means. We take the training-data debate seriously and are happy to talk about it."

**Track everything** in the Status / Next columns of `refs/outreach-tracker.md`.

## 8. Readiness checklist (the gate to Phase 2)

Have most of these green before spending a gallery's attention:

- [ ] **Body of work**, ~15-25 cohesive pieces in the series (not ~7).
- [ ] **Artist website** live with curatorial statements.
- [ ] **Consistent Instagram** with a coherent grid.
- [ ] **Permissionless presence**, fxhash/objkt, works minted + 1-3 sales.
- [ ] **Third-party validation**, 1-2: an open call/biennale, a feature, or a prize shortlist.
- [ ] **A press hit** on the project (Kaloh / Right Click Save).
- [ ] **Project statement**, one paragraph + Vince bio + your framing.
- [ ] **Press kit**, 8-10 best images, statement, links.
- [ ] **First sales on record**, even small.
