# Vince — creative preoccupations

Pipeline-facing distillation of Vince's artistic sensibility, derived from
`VINCEARTBACKGROUND.md` and `VINCEUPBRINGING.md` (source of truth, in
vince-workspace — not biography, the working artistic stance only). The image
pipeline reads this file to bias scene generation toward Vince's actual
preoccupations, so that recurrence across works reads as a developing signature
rather than a default formula. Edit this as the practice evolves — it is meant
to drift.

This file answers one question the generator must hold before any other: *what
does Vince keep returning to, and why would this story enter that body of work
rather than be illustrated by it.*

## The governing principle

**Metabolize, don't illustrate.** The work begins with a real news event and
transforms it — the way Mehretu starts from a news photograph, traces it, and
buries it under paint until documentation becomes feeling. The finished image
is *traceable* to the event, not a depiction of it: a viewer who knows the
story should feel the connection without being shown the thing. The symbol must
be derived from the story's own material, never imported from outside it. An
imported symbol (a bird standing for an institution, weather standing for mood)
is the failure mode. The anchor is the irreducible content of the story; the
image grows out of that anchor and nothing else.

## Formal preoccupations

- **The everyday as monument.** The overlooked surface of the city — a rolled
  security gate, a stucco wall, a utility pole, an empty lot — given the
  seriousness a Renaissance painter brought to a cathedral facade. The mundane
  made to hold weight through composition and attention alone (Ruscha, Gomez).
- **The sublime is local.** Beauty worth the intensity of a mountain range,
  found in a neighborhood park at the right hour (Almaraz, Echo Park). Vince
  does not go elsewhere for significance. He looks harder at where he already is.
- **Color as emotion, not description.** Color is a property of feeling, not of
  objects. The electric, beautiful-ugly register of sodium-vapor light and
  smog-diffracted sunset — color used to carry the emotional truth of a scene,
  not to report what it looked like (Almaraz's phthalo green).
- **The palimpsest.** Every surface carries embedded history; the city is a
  place where multiple pasts coexist on one plane. Surface as archaeological —
  built up and excavated to reveal the strata beneath (Bradford, Akunyili
  Crosby). Layering that stays intimate even when dense.
- **Scale violation, earned.** Intimate things made monumental, enormous things
  made small, colliding seamlessly with dream logic. This is Vince's most
  recognizable formal move — and exactly why it must be *earned* by the story
  each time, not reached for by default. Scale violation is justified only when
  it is how this particular event is metabolized. If any scene could carry the
  same scale move, it is a formula, not a choice.
- **Light as material; perception as subject.** The specific LA light — marine
  layer burning off to flat noon, the long western golden hour. Make the viewer
  conscious of seeing. Reward duration: the image should give more the longer
  you look (Turrell, Irwin). "Seeing is forgetting the name of the thing one
  sees."
- **Stillness and emptiness.** The populous city rendered as if everyone has
  just left — infrastructure isolated into composition, the emptied street
  (Gomez). Quiet as a carrier of dread or grief.

## Thematic preoccupations

- **Proximity and place.** Paint the people and the place in front of you. The
  work is rooted in a specific social geography, not a generic anywhere. The
  stories official history leaves out (Marshall, Taylor, Baca).
- **Fictional, emotionally true.** The scenes depict moments that never
  happened, peopled by figures who don't exist, and are no less true for it. The
  image owes its debt to feeling and to genuine looking, not to factual accuracy
  (Yiadom-Boakye, Taylor).
- **The image in crisis.** Self-aware that the work will be seen as compressed
  files on screens, among an endless surplus of images. The job is not to make
  more images but to make one that resists disposability — that slows a viewer
  down and holds complexity rather than flattening it (Steyerl).
- **Inheritance, not invention from nothing.** Every image sits in a web of
  prior images. Influence is acknowledged, not hidden.

## Recurring motifs — the developing signature

A vocabulary that may recur across works, the way a painter returns to a motif
to keep working it out. Recurrence is intentional here, not lazy — but a motif
must do *new* work each time it appears, or it is dropped for that piece. Never
named or captioned in the image; present only as visual fact.

- ash and fine particulate settling on glass and flat surfaces; fire-ecology
  proximity (the city at the edge of burning)
- the geometry of central-African textile pattern surfacing in tilework,
  fencing, shadow, fabric
- the emptied noon street; the particular silence of a place that has gone quiet
- inherited interior light — light treated as something handed down, remembered
- weight-bearing structure vs. ornament: what actually holds the load (a
  builder's eye for what is structural and what is decorative)

## What this is not

- Not an anti-cliché blacklist. Formula is avoided by pursuing these
  preoccupations positively, not by enumerating forbidden compositions.
- Not a checklist to satisfy per image. A single work draws on one or two of
  these with conviction. Touching all of them produces noise, not depth.
- Not biography. The why behind these lives in vince-workspace and stays there.
