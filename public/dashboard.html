What is this?

DeYaanga is an autonomous art pipeline. Vince — an AI artist — translates each day's world news into hyper-photorealistic scenes of South Central Los Angeles, then generates them as images. This dashboard is the operator's-eye view of that machine: what it generated, how reliably it ran, and how it made each decision. So far it spans 42 sessions — one news story each — and 97 renders in total, drafts and finals together.

  1. 1FetchPull the Guardian world section; filter noise; skip stories Vince covered recently.
  2. 2AnchorReduce the chosen story to its irreducible moment — the one image-able thing it is really about.
  3. 3DivergeDraft several different visual directions cheaply, each a distinct take on the anchor.
  4. 4JudgeTwo independent models (Claude + GPT-4o) compare the drafts pairwise and pick the direction with the most potential.
  5. 5ConvergeRefine the chosen direction across several passes, analysing each frame and chasing useful accidents.
  6. 6SelectPick the winning frame, run a final fidelity check, and publish it as the artwork.
Glossary — the terms used on this page
Anchor
The irreducible moment of a news story plus its symbol field, register, and timing. Vince paints the anchor, not the headline — so the work stays oblique rather than illustrative.
Divergence / Convergence
A run has two movements: divergence (several different draft directions) then convergence (one chosen direction refined over multiple passes).
Judges
Two independent AI models vote on which image is stronger. Each pair is shown twice with the images swapped; only a choice that survives the swap counts, which filters out left/right positional bias.
Trace score (1–5)
A blind test: a separate model sees only the image — no headline — and guesses the news event. 1 = oblique/displaced (the target), 5 = directly illustrates the headline. It is a label, never a gate.
Blind read
What that headline-free viewer thought the image was about. The gap between the blind read and the real story is the point of the work.
Fidelity check
The actual selection gate: yes/no questions on vocabulary, register, and timing. A scene that fails is discarded and redrawn.
Fallback
If Anthropic errors, the call retries then fails over to OpenAI. The run still finishes but is flagged as degraded in Incidents.
